What is GST ‚Äì Goods and Services taxes

This is a type of tax levied on goods and services which are used by common people. 10% value was kept fixed for this tax. GST was brought to Australia by Howard Liberal Government on 1st July 2000. There are many items and services which are exempted from GST such as medical, education services, exports, some basic food items, government charges, or outside the scope of GST. The revenue collected by the government by imposing GST is distributed in all states of Australia.

In short, the 10% GST is charged on almost every service or good used or consumed in Australia. If you are registered for GST, you can charge 10% extra as a GST from your customers for services or goods they purchased from you, and this is called Sales GST.

You can also claim GST, how? Simply by claiming GST for those items and services which you used for your business expenses, and this claimed GST is known as GST credit. To calculate your GST, we recommend you use our Australia GST calculator, which is very easy to use.

There are 2 different types of sales in relation to GST:

  1. Suppliers of GST-free goods and services will not have to pay GST when they make a sale, but they will be entitled to GST credits.
  2. Suppliers of input-taxed goods and services do not have to charge GST on sales, but they will not be entitled to claim GST credits from their purchases of inputs.
